About

This My Nature Outing project inquires into questions surrounding sustainable design and examines local efforts at the University of Pittburgh. My Nature Outing is sponsored by the University of Pittsburgh's John C. Mascaro Center for Sustainable Engineering and the Office of the Provost's Year of Data and Society.

Thriftsburgh

Thriftsburgh is a clothing thriftstore donated by students to then be sold inexpensively to other students who may not have the funds to buy more expensive clothing. This idea for a campus thriftstore is genius; its giving students a way to donate clothing they dont want while also conveniently allowing students to buy new clothing on campus.

The Bike Cave is a workshop to repair and restore old and broken bikes that were donated. They then sell the improved bikes, mostly aimed to college students to get around on campus. These bikes are very inexpensive, ranging from 20$-120$. Anywhere could have Bike Caves; anyone can donate and any bike can be repaired to sell to people. In addition to being inexpensive and helpful to students, riding bikes also improves the environment.

Natural rain drains are strategically placed plants to use rainwater and to help flooding. This is important to use excess rainwater but to also provide pollinating insects more plants. These Natural Rain Drains are convenient and can be easily placed on sidewalks.
