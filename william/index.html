About

This My Nature Outing project inquires into questions surrounding sustainable design and examines local efforts at the University of Pittburgh. My Nature Outing is sponsored by the University of Pittsburgh's John C. Mascaro Center for Sustainable Engineering and the Office of the Provost's Year of Data and Society.

Rain Gardens collect water and drain it so that places and sewers don't get flooded. What happends is that the plants in the garden absorb the rain water so that the large amount of it doesn't go into the sewer system, flood it, and pollute the rivers. Its important becuause Pittsburgh has a lot of rain and if we had no Rain Gardens, all the water would go into the sewers and flood them. Then that polluted water would go into the rivers and contaminate them and the things that live in them. We could inprove this by making the size of the gardens bigger so they can absorb more water.
